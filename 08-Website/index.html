Combo Captures

Each piece is a loyal little dog-soldier (yes — the old players of Tataouine always called them "the dogs" 🐶). Trap an enemy between two of yours and it falls — then keep going. One dog-soldier can chain through three, four, five captures in a single turn: DOUBLE → TRIPLE → ONSLAUGHT → ANNIHILATION — with yelps, screen-shake and slow-mo. From Roman Legions to Your Pocket

530 BCE

A Greek vase shows Achilles at the board — Petteia, the ancestor.

1st c. BCE

Rome plays Ludus Latrunculorum — "the game of soldiers." Legionaries play it on Hadrian's Wall.

11th c.

Carried across North Africa, it takes root in the Tunisian south.

Today

Elders still draw it in the sand of Tataouine. Now reborn by Kogia Games.
