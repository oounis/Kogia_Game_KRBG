Drop 2 soldiers to begin the placement phase.

Placement Phase: Alternate placing 2 soldiers. Center Citadel must stay empty. Once all 48 soldiers are placed, the play phase starts!

How to play

Move

Slide a soldier one square orthogonally — up, down, left or right. Never diagonally.

Surround

Trap an enemy between two of yours on a line (custodial capture), and it's captured.

Multi-capture

A single move can capture several enemies in adjacent lines.

Mullah

Your 7th capture promotes that soldier to a Mullah — it slides any distance orthogonally to strike.

The rules, in full

  • The board. 7×7. Two armies of soldiers; the centre square (the Citadel) starts empty.
  • Moving. One soldier per turn moves a single square orthogonally into an empty cell.
  • Capturing. Move so an enemy is sandwiched between two of your soldiers on a row or column — it's removed. A move may trap several at once.
  • Safe. A soldier that moves itself between two enemies is not captured.
  • The Mullah. When one of your soldiers makes your 7th capture, it is promoted to a Mullah: it slides any number of empty squares in a straight line orthogonally, capturing by the sandwich rule.
  • The Sultan. Each player has one Sultan. It slides any number of empty squares orthogonally from the start. If your Sultan is captured (sandwiched), you lose immediately!
  • Winning. Reduce the enemy to one soldier, capture their Sultan, or leave them with no legal move.
  • Pure skill. No dice, no cards, no luck — traditional North African game of strategy.
