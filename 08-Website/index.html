01

Older than chess.
Pure as mathematics.

Kharbga descends from the Roman Ludus Latrunculorum — "the game of soldiers" — a favourite of legionaries from Carthage to Hadrian's Wall. For centuries it lived on in the Tunisian south, drawn in sand, played with stones.

It belongs to the noble no-luck family of chess and Go: no dice, no chance. You win by thinking further than your rival. We kept that soul exactly — and forged a modern edge.

02

How it works

Move

Slide a soldier one square — orthogonally, never diagonally.

Surround

Trap an enemy between two of yours on a line, and it falls.

Chain

One capture lets the same soldier strike again — combo!

Crown

Seven captures and your soldier becomes a Centurion.
