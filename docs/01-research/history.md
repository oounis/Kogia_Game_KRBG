# Kharbga — Historical Research

> Seed research for the game's History section and cultural-preservation angle. Sourced; expand with
> field interviews from Tataouine elders before publishing (see regional-variations.md).

## The lineage — a 2,500-year-old game
Kharbga belongs to one of the oldest families of pure-strategy (no-luck) board games:

1. **Petteia (Greek)** — an ancient Greek game of position and capture; depicted on a famous ~530 BCE
   vase showing Achilles and Ajax at the board.
2. **Ludus Latrunculorum (Roman)** — Rome adapted Petteia into the *"game of soldiers / brigands."*
   Referenced by Ovid, Varro and others from the 1st c. BCE. Archaeology shows it was hugely popular
   with **Roman soldiers** — in Roman Britain ~59% of found gaming boards are this game, concentrated at
   forts along Hadrian's Wall. A cerebral "battlefield" with **no dice, no chance** — pure tactics.
3. **The Seega family (North Africa / Egypt)** — custodial-capture games spread across Egypt, Sudan,
   Somalia and the Maghreb. Seega is the 5×5 cousin; Kharbga is the 7×7 southern-Tunisian form.
4. **Kharbga (Tunisia)** — carried across North Africa (tradition links it to the Banu Hilal migrations,
   11th c.) and kept alive for centuries in the Tunisian south — Tataouine, Médenine, Gafsa, Djerba.

## How it lived
Traditionally **drawn in the sand or on asphalt with charcoal**, and played with whatever was at hand:
two colours of pebbles, **date seeds (نواة التمر)**, white shells (**البابوش الأبيض**), or bottle caps.
Men gathered in the shade and in cafés — the game was as much about **community and conversation** as
competition. It had nearly faded with the pace of modern life and screens; reviving it is the heart of
this project.

## Why it matters for the product
This is not "just another board game clone." It is a **living piece of North-African heritage** with a
documented 2,500-year pedigree. That story is:
- the **emotional hook** for Tunisian/Maghreb players and the diaspora,
- the **SEO and press angle** (cultural preservation, not a generic app),
- and the reason the History section deserves to be a first-class part of the website.

## ⚠️ Source caution
- **kharbga.com / Kharbga Game Network** — the most faithful online rules (southern-Tunisia version). Primary.
- **Wikipedia "Kharbaga"** — a *different/variant* spelling and ruleset; do **not** treat as canonical.
- **"Tunisia (board game)"** (BoardGameGeek) — a commercialised Western product inspired by the family,
  not the traditional game. Useful as reference, not as the rules source.
- **Always verify** board/piece counts and the Al-Haf rule against living players in Tataouine before publishing.
