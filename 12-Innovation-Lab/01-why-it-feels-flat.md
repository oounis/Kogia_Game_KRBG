# Why Classic Kharbga Feels Flat (and the lever to fix each)

Honest diagnosis of what makes the traditional game drag — and whether the fix is *rules*, *AI*, or *presentation*.

| # | What feels boring | Why | Main fix |
|---|-------------------|-----|----------|
| 1 | **Long, abstract placement** (you drop 24 pieces before anything "happens") | No feedback, no tension, all upfront | **Rules** — pre-set formations or a quick draft instead of 24 manual drops |
| 2 | **All pieces are identical** | No piece "personalities" → far fewer tactical patterns than chess's 6 types | **Rules** — add a *few* special pieces (the big lever) |
| 3 | **One repeated verb** (sandwich, sandwich, sandwich) | Chess has forks/pins/skewers; Kharbga has one capture idea | **Rules** — new objectives + special pieces create new tactics |
| 4 | **No focal target** | Chess aims at ONE king (drama, "check!"); Kharbga = grind down 24 anonymous pieces | **Rules** — add a *royal* piece (capture the Sheikh = win) |
| 5 | **Weak/greedy AI** | The bot doesn't punish mistakes or set traps → no challenge, no learning curve | **AI** — minimax/alpha-beta with a real board evaluation + difficulty ladder |
| 6 | **No game-feel ("juice")** | No animation, sound, capture flash, hints → feels lifeless even when the position is rich | **Presentation** — animations, sfx, capture VFX, last-move highlight, undo, hints |
| 7 | **Endgame drags** | Capturing the final few pieces is tedious | **Rules** — win at "≤2 pieces left" / a Sheikh capture ends it sooner |
| 8 | **No progression / reasons to return** | One-off match vs a bot; nothing to chase | **Meta** — AI ladder (The Kid → Sheikh), daily puzzle, ranks, online later |

## The takeaway
~Half of "boring" is **AI + juice + UX** (cheap, universal wins — do these regardless).
The other half is **rules depth** — and the single highest-impact change is **piece variety + a royal target** (see `02`).
Both together turn a flat grind into something with chess-like drama, while the capture core stays pure Kharbga.
