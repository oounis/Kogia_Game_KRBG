# Naming & "Is Kharbga like Go?"

## Part 1 - Is Kharbga really similar to Go?
**Partly - but it's actually closer to chess/checkers than to Go.**

| | **Go** | **Kharbga** | **Chess** |
|---|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Pieces move after placing? | no (mostly static) | **yes, you move** | yes |
| Capture by... | surrounding (removing liberties) | **sandwiching** between 2 (custodial) | attacking the square |
| Goal | control **territory** | **eliminate / block** the enemy | checkmate the king |
| Piece types | 1 (stones) | 1 (soldiers) | 6 |
| Luck | none | none | none |

**Verdict:** Kharbga *looks* Go-ish (grid + surround + no luck), but mechanically it's a **move-and-capture**
game - the Latrunculorum/checkers family. The Go feeling is the **"surround to win" DNA**.
The sweet spot for Origins Mode = borrow **one** thing from each: **Go's territory** (the Citadel-as-prize)
+ **chess's royal target & piece variety** (the Centurion + specials).

---

## Part 2 - A short, memorable, global name (English / Roman only)
You want something easy like "Chess" or "Go", with no Arabic. Reality check: **don't over-optimize the name
now** - a great game with an ok name beats the reverse, and you can A/B test on the website later.

### Direction 1 - keep Kharbga + a global subtitle (recommended for heritage)
- **Kharbga: Origins** (keeps the one authentic word + SEO + the "preservation" story)
- Kharbga: Legends - Kharbga: Sands

### Direction 2 - a short Roman/Latin brand (easy like "Go", fits the Roman root)
- **LATRO** - Latin for the game's own soldier-piece ("the soldier/mercenary"). Short, authentic, ownable.
- **LATRUNCULI** - the Roman game's actual name. Epic, but longer.
- **NUMIDA** - from the Numidian desert warriors. Sounds global and ancient.
- **CASTRA** - the Roman military camp. Short, strong, martial.
- **CENTURION** - our promoted piece; could double as the brand.

### Recommendation
Lead with **Kharbga: Origins** (heritage + SEO). If you ever want a short standalone brand, **LATRO** or
**NUMIDA** are the strongest Roman options. Decide at launch with real audience feedback - keep the repo name for now.
